Repreive for Waiguru as Court temporarily halts ouster plan

Kirinyaga Governor Anne Waiguru yesterday got a repreive after a court temporarily halted her impeachment process. High court
judge Weldon Korir ruled that the county assembly shold stop the ouster plan until the coronavirus pandemic is contained

Mr Korir said holding the impeachment proceeding at this time will prejudice Governor Waiguru's political rights. "In my view,
the unique circumstances posed by the Covid-19 pandemic imposes a duty to the court to interfere with the county
assembly's mandate in as far as the impeachment proceeding is concerned; the governor's rights are threatened as
the doctrine of public participation will be violated," the court ruled.

Ms Waiguru had argued that she is complying with the national government's directive for people to work from home.
The judge noted that impeachment plan requires the governor and her lawyer to present themselves before the assembly.
The court said it is evident that public participation will be affected by the pandemic yet residents must
be involved through qualitative and quantitive participation

Five MPs lash out at Tuju for 'meddling' in assembly operations

Five Jubilee MPs have lashed out at a party secretary-general Raphael Tuju for allegedly interfering with the operations of
the Kirinyaga County Assembly.They accused Mr Tuju of intimidating ward reps over their plan to impeach Ms Waiguru.

Mr Tuju had asked MCAs to first exhaust all internal party dispute-resolution mechanisms before filing an impeachment motion,
warning that such a move would impinge on the party's national agenda. Speaking in Sagana, the MPs said the majority leader
was elected by MCAs and only they can remove him office. "We have also seen a letter from the secretary-general which
appears to intimidate all jubilee MCAs, particularly the nominated ones," Mr Wambugu said.
