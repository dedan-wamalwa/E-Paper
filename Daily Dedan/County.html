Stakeholders want action taken before next planting season in Agricultural areas

Residents of Laikipia county are staring at a food crisis after their crops were destroyed by desert locusts and wild animals.
The county is home to plantations where some of the country's best agricultural efforts have been demonstrated.
Among the areas that throughout the years have been receiving bumper harvests, producing horticultural
produce, wheat, maize and other cereals are Ng'arua , Marmanet, Igwamiti, Sipili, Salama and Sironi.

As a substantial agricultural county, we were expecting a bumper harvest this season because we had received favourable
rains last year but we have however lost alot of our farm produce to desert locusts" said Ms Rebeca Wambui,
a farmer in Shamanei. The farmers-who claimed they had been left to fight the swarms of locusts, which
are still ravaging some farms, with little help from the government-say the insects now pose
a danger to food production in the region.

Locusts continue to ravage parts of North Rift as the government shifts its attention to fighting Covid-19 pandemic posing
a threat to food security of the country. Kenya Farmer's Association director Kipkorir Menjo said now that the focus
had shifted to the pandemic, swarms spotted in Kapseret, Moiben and Ainabkoi recently if they continue will
lead to a food crisis if nothing is done. Turkana County is on high alert for a possible invasion, nine months
after the devolved unit spent Sh27 million to control the insects across its six constituencies.
