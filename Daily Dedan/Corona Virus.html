WEARING A MASK KEY TO FIGHTING CORONAVIRUS

WHO has revised its stand, saying masks can limit spread of the viral disease

With the daily spike in the cases of the corona virus, wearing of masks-in addition to other preventive measures like staying at home,
handwashing and keeping social distance-might help slow down the spread of covid-19.

Yesterday,the World Health Organization (WHO) revised its stand on masks, acknowledging that wearing a mask is one of
the prevention measures to limit the spread of the virus.The revision came after months of dithering on the issue despite
mounting evidence that countries that adopted the use of masks for everyone,such as China, Japan, Thailand, South Korea and
Taiwan, have slowed down the lethal itinerary of covid-19 as compared to the United States and Europe. They recorded fewer
infections and deaths.


WHO Boss Tedros Adhanos has revised their earlier stance on wearing masks

Even before WHO's advisory,Health Cabinet Secretary had called on Kenyans to wear masks outdoors to stop the spread of the virus.
Dr. Patrick Oyaro, an epidemiologist and a public health expert, explained how face masks prevent diseases.

He told the Nation that masks are "a physical barrier" which catch tiny organisms (microbes) that the sick person may expel to the
environment. Masks also protect the healthy from inhaling the germs in the environment

More evidence is also coming that covid-19 could be airborne,and that mere act of talking and breathing releases this pathogenic
germs to the air which one can breathe and get infected.
